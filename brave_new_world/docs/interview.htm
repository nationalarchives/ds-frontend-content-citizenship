Censorship in the theatre, 1965
This is an extract from an interview with the Lord Chamberlain, Lord Cobbold, which formed part of the evidence submitted to the House of Lords and House of Commons joint inquiry into theatre censorship. Since the 18th century the Lord Chamberlain had powers to license plays and theatres. All new plays had to be presented to him for approval, but this power was seen as increasingly out of date in the 20th century. The Theatre Act of 1968 ended censorship of plays.
