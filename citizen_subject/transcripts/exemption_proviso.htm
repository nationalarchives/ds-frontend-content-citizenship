Proviso to the 1450 Act of Resumption
Catalogue reference: C 49/58, no. 45
Also foreseyn and provyded yat this present Act of Resumption nether extende nor strecche in any Wyse to ye graunte made by us to John Bury. By the name / of John Bury yeoman usher of our halle. Of the office of Sergeaunt of Armes Wt xij d on the day to be perceived by hym yerely for terme. Of his lyfe of the ferme of ye countees of London & Midd And of the issuz and profytz of the said Schires coming by the hondes of ye Schirefs of the said Schyres for the tyme beynge. Withour livery of clothing to be take yerly by hym at oure grete Warderobe for terme of his lyve by the handez of the keeper of our said warderobe for the tyme being nor to the hurte or preiudice of the seid John in eny Wyse touching ye seid grauntz

it is thought resonable in so meche as he attendeth upon ye speker of ye parlement

Modern English

Also foresaid and provided that this present Act of Resumption neither extend nor stretch in any wise to the grant made by us to John Bury, by the name of John Bury, usher of our hall, of the office of sergeant of arms with 12d on the day to be perceived by him yearly for term of his life of the farm of the counties of London and Middlesex, and of the issues and profits of the said shires coming by the hands of the sheriffs of the said shires for the time being, without livery of clothing to be taken yearly by him at our great wardrobe for term of his life by the hands of the keeper of our said wardrobe for the time being nor to the hurt or prejudice of the same John in any wise touching the said grants.

It is thought reasonable in so much as he attends daily on the speaker of the parliament.
