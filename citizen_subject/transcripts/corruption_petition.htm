Petition to the Royal Council alleging corruption
in the election of knights of the shire for Suffolk, 1453
Catalogue reference: E 28/84/24

To the right honourable and most discret councell
of the kyng oure soverayn lord

Sheweth un to your right honourable and wyse discrecyous John Duc of Norff that where atte shire for to be holden at yepeswych in the shire of Suff the munday next after the fest of seynt volantyne last passed ther come diverse servauntz and tenauntz of the seide Duc to the eleccon of the knyghtes of the shire for the last parlement ther for to have yeven ther voyse to the knyghtes of the shire after the lawe of this londe There Thomas Sharnburn at that tyme Sherreve of the seide shire ymagynyng and purposing to make knyghtes of the shire aftyr hys owne intent and for hys syngler coveytyse and to hurt the servauntz and tenauntz of the seide Duc hath retorned in the comen place a rescous of many and divers servantz and tenauntz of the seid Duc Whose names be annexed to this bylle surmyttyng be hys retourn that hys officers durst not holde the shire aforeseide be cause of manas and thretes made to hys seide officers as he retorned in the common plase afore seide Where that the seid servauntz and tenauntz of the seid Duc made never no suych maner of fors rescous ner mannas as the seide Shirreve hath returned but that thei pesibley kome to the shire hous for the eleccion of the knyghtes of the shire after the lawe of theis londe Where fore pleast it youre right wyse and noble discreycyous to sende oure soverayn lordys letters to the Justicez of the comen place comaundyng them to admytte all the servauntz and tenauntz of the seide Duc conteyned in the seide bylle to apper by attorne and to answer by attorne considering them to admytte alle the servauntz and tenauntz Which ar innocent persones be returned in the seide rescous A yens al maner reson and consciens of the which diverse and many were not there at that tyme as it shal suffisauntly be proved atte reverens of god in the day of charitie

W Ebor Salisbury Worcestre Bourgchier
W Winton R St Johns
D S Elien Dudley
F Norwici

Modern English

E 28/84/24

To the right honourable and most discreet council
Of the king our sovereign lord

Show unto your right honourable and wise discreet John Duke of Norfolk that where at the shire (court) to be held at Ipswich in the shire of Suffolk the Monday next after the feast of St Valentine last past there came diverse tenants and servants of the said Duke to the election of the knights of the shire for the last parliament there for to have given their voice to the knights of the shire after the law of this land. There Thomas Sharnburn, at that time sheriff of the said shire imagining and proposing to make knights of the shire after his own intent and for his singular covetousness and to hurt the servants and tenants of the said Duke has returned in the Common Pleas a 'rescous' of many and diverse servants and tenants of the said Duke whose names are annexed to this bill, surmising by his return that his officers dare not hold the shire [court] previously said because of menace and threats made to his said officers as he returned in the Common Pleas previously said. Where that the said servants and tenants of the said Duke never made any such manner of force, rescue or menace as the said sheriff has returned but that they peacefully came to the shire house for the election of the knights of the shire after the law of the land. Wherefore please it your right wise and noble discreet to send our sovereign lords letters to the Justices of the Common Pleas commanding them to admit all the servants and tenants of the said Duke contained in the said bill to appear by attorney and to answer by attorney, considering them to admit all the servants and tenants which are innocent persons [to] be returned in the said 'rescous' against all manner of reason and conscience of the which diverse and many were not there at that time as it shall sufficiently be proved at the reverence of God in the day of charity.

W York Salisbury Worcester Bourchier
W Winchester R St Johns
D S Ely Dudley
F Norwich
