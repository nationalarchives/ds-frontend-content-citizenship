Proviso to the 1450 Act of Resumption

In addition to its other roles, Parliament was a place where private Acts and interests could be protected, preserved and endorsed.

Acts of Resumption recalled and annulled grants made by previous monarchs, and sometimes these Acts included hundreds of provisos. Kings such as Edward IV often interviewed claimants personally before their provisos were granted. In this case, John Bury's claim was thought reasonable because he was a servant of the speaker of that particular Parliament.
Catalogue reference: C 49/58, no. 45 (1450)
