The Stamp Act, 1765
These are the opening paragraphs of the Stamp Act - a key legislative document in defining relations between Britain and America. When the Bill was introduced into the House of Commons, by George Grenville, it was clearly asserted that the British Parliament had a moral authority to levy the tax. But once it was passed, the Act provoked an intense political debate that focused initially on the issue of parliamentary representation and then expanded into questions of sovereignty. In America, the legislation met with defiance and the tax proved impossible to collect.
